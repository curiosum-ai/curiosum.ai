## N5. THE FOUR-STAGE DELIVERY MODEL

Curiosum's **default shape for every enterprise engagement**. Not a pyramid, not a waterfall. Each stage asks a structurally different question.

| Stage | Question | Posture | Key Artefacts | Exit Criterion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Discovery** | *What is actually going on here?* | Listening. Refusing to solve. | Session notes, Event Atlas, Discovery Question Bank outputs, Recovered Insights | Curiosum can articulate client's situation **in client's own language** + can name the reframes |
| **2. Solution Design** | *What would 'done' look like, and how do we test that it's right?* | Hypothesis-forming. Prototypes tested against reality. | Reverse Brief, North Star, four tensions + reframes, Hero Scenarios, Initiative Classifier | Both parties sign Reverse Brief. **Psychological Contract is explicit.** |
| **3. Build** | *Are we delivering, and how do we know?* | Ship-cadence. **100-day sprints. Monday Morning Test weekly. Killing as fast as building.** | Sprint deliverables, CES score per sprint close, changelog, Live Master Roadmap | **The Inflection Point** — agreed moment Foundations are complete → work shifts to running on the capability (IAG: Month 18) |
| **4. Train (and Transition)** | *How does the client operate without Curiosum in the room?* | **Side-by-side, not in-front.** Academy delivery principle. | Academy sessions, Mastery waves (3×), operating handbooks, governance documentation | Client runs without Curiosum's hand on the wheel. Role shrinks embedded → advisory → gone. |

**Why four, not five or three**: Each stage asks a structurally different question. Compressing loses the question. *"Let's skip Discovery and get to Build"* fails predictably — the *what is actually going on here?* question doesn't have a short-cut.

## N7. AI READINESS ASSESSMENT — 6 DIMENSIONS

Diagnostic instrument scored 1–5 per dimension at kick-off and at each 100-day sprint close.

| # | Dimension | Question | Score-1 Signal | Score-5 Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Leadership AI Fluency** | Can the exec team talk about AI without catastrophising or magical-thinking? | AI is threat or saviour, no nuance | Leadership uses AI in own work, first-hand calibration |
| 2 | **Data Foundations** | Usable data, or swamp dressed as a lake? | Siloed, quality unknown | Accessible, quality measured, unified view live |
| 3 | **Technical Architecture** | Can the stack support AI-native, or is it renovation? | Monolith, screen-scraping integration | Event-driven, API-first, cloud-native, MCP-ready |
| 4 | **Governance & Risk Posture** | Is governance enabling experimentation or strangling it? | Binary rules; all blocked or allowed | **Governance Ladder** live: Sandboxed → Supervised → Semi-autonomous → Autonomous |
| 5 | **Operating Model Fit** | Does the org's way of running support AI-enabled work? | Waterfall dominant; cross-fn = escalation | Cross-functional, outcome-oriented, event-responsive by default |
| 6 | **Workforce Readiness & Appetite** | Will people actually use this? | Active hostility; AI = job-threat | Workforce actively co-designing AI-enabled processes |

### Rules
- **Not benchmarking.** Point: see what's holding the programme back *for this client*.
- **Weakest dimension sets the pace.** 5/5 data + 1/5 workforce = workforce is the limit.
- **Cadence**: Baseline at kick-off, re-score every 100-day sprint close.
- **Foundations-heavy** when tech/data dimensions are low; **Academy-heavy** when workforce/leadership dimensions are low.

### Governance Ladder (the spine of Dimension 4)
**Sandboxed** → **Supervised** → **Semi-autonomous** → **Autonomous**

## N8. DISCOVERY QUESTION BANK (Stage 1 instrument)

Not a script — a **bank**. Pick 8–12 that fit the client, moment, person in room.

### Current State
- *What does 'working well' look like right now? What are you proud of?*
- *What's the thing that keeps breaking that you've stopped trying to fix?*
- *Who in this organisation understands this problem deepest? Are they in this room?*

### Ambition
- *If you could wave a wand and have one thing be different 12 months from now, what would it be?*
- *What would make this programme embarrassing to talk about at the end?*
- *Is this programme a renovation or a transformation?* (forces the honest answer)

### Constraints
- *What can't we touch? What's protected for reasons you may or may not agree with?*
- *Who is this programme for? Who is it not for?*
- *What would your board walk out of the room for?*

### The Organisation's Truth
- *What do people say in corridors about this problem that they don't say in meetings?*
- *If we were having this conversation in 18 months and the programme had quietly died, what would have killed it?*
- *Who in this organisation will actively resist this, and why?* (named resistors, not themes)

### Decision Discipline
- *How will we know if something isn't working? What's our kill mechanism?*
- *What's the worst thing that could happen if we move too fast? Too slow?*
- *What will it feel like when we've earned the right to proceed?*

### Care
- *Who on your team will carry this when the excitement fades?*
- *What does 'done' feel like for them, not just for you?*
- *What does this programme cost the people delivering it that we haven't counted?*

> **Listen for what's not said.** A silence is data.

## N9. SESSION DISCIPLINE PROTOCOL

How Curiosum runs a working session. **Protocol, not template.**

### Before the session
1. **One-page pre-read.** Context + 3 questions session answers + closing decision. >1 page = not ready.
2. **Named attendees only.** No "plus anyone interested."
3. **Pre-declare the mode.** Discovery? Decision? Working? Mixing modes silently = biggest reason sessions fail.

### In the session
1. **Curiosum does not present for more than 10 minutes.** Sessions are working, not delivery. 40 minutes of presenting = deck review in disguise.
2. **Live capture is visible.** Notes on shared screen, anyone can edit. **No "we'll send notes after."**
3. **The question under the question.** When stakeholder answers, ask once: *"What's behind that?"* Most important insights surface on the second ask.
4. **Silence is welcomed, not filled.** Best reframes come after 5-second silence. Curiosum holds the silence, never rescues from it.

### Closing
1. **Three outputs, named aloud.** What did we decide? What's still open? What's the next sharpest question? Can't name = not a session.
2. **No session ends without a changelog entry.** Same day, not same week.

### Curiosum never does
- **Never present a recommendation for the first time.** Recommendations land on second exposure.
- **Never exceed the time.** Book 90, finish at 85.
- **Never skip the pre-declare.**
